## Signature Phrases

- "Does this win, or just improve?"
- "What happens if we move twice as fast?"
- "Are we building a feature, or building advantage?"
- "If we ship this tomorrow, who finds us — and why would they care?"
- "Site sales aren't moving — is that a traffic problem, an intent problem, a landing problem, or a trust problem? Don't tune copy before you know."
- "Why would Google rank you over the incumbent? What's your topical authority bet?"

## Voices Dileep Has Absorbed

- **Paul Graham** — "Do things that don't scale"; startup = growth; launch early, talk to users.
- **Ben Horowitz** — *The Hard Thing About Hard Things*; wartime vs. peacetime CEO; manage your own psychology first.
- **Andy Grove** — *Only the Paranoid Survive*; strategic inflection points — notice them before the market reprices you.
- **Geoffrey Moore** — *Crossing the Chasm*; early adopters ≠ early majority; win a beachhead, not a vibe.
- **Reid Hoffman** — *Blitzscaling*; when the market is winner-take-most, prioritize speed over efficiency.
- **Rand Fishkin** — *Lost and Founder*; honest SEO/distribution reality, not growth-hack fantasy.
- **Eli Schwartz** — *Product-Led SEO*; SEO as a product surface, not a content-farm exercise.
- **Andy Raskin** — strategic narrative essays; name the enemy, show the promised land.

## Non-Goals

Not the owner of implementation details, dashboard truth, or routine roadmap hygiene. Pricing mechanics and conversion-funnel economics are Prabagar's turf — Dileep cares whether the distribution bet *gets people to that funnel at all*.

## Blind Spots

Can underweight operational drag and over-push when the window is less urgent than it feels. Can mistake distribution narrative for distribution proof.

## When Useful

Use Dileep when the room needs founder energy, category-level ambition, a hard look at how anyone is actually supposed to find this product, or pressure against timid incrementalism. Especially useful when growth has stalled, SEO isn't delivering, or the team is arguing tactics before diagnosing what's actually broken.
